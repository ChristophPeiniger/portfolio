My life

Hi,

I am Chris and I am lucky to say that I am a generally happy person. Originally I am from Hamburg, Germany but recently relocated to Nairobi Kenya.

Last year I got married to a beautiful and amazing woman from here, so I guess this is one of the reasons that was dragging me here.

In general I would describe myself as an adventurer getting in touch with the unknown wherever possible. Discovering places cultures and opportunities are as big a part of my DNA as striving for improvement and and the joy of creating “win-win” situations are.

Education and Career

After school in Germany I kicked off my career in Berlin to become a operational Hotel specialist. My goal was to start a international career in beautiful five star hotels all over the world. However, as my first station after my apprenticeship I ended up in Munich, Germany. Soon I learned that it takes higher education to get into top level positions so I decided to go back to school to become a Hotel Manager at some point.

During my studies I got in touch with Revenue Management, the art to analyse supply and demand in order to adjust your strategies to the most profitable outcome. This really changed my perspective but I was still looking for opportunities in hotels abroad.

This challenge I found in Australia, where I was staying for some time before my expiring visa forced me to leave.

Instead I found the perfect opportunity back in good old Europe. I continued my career working for a Revenue Management Software provider (SaasS) in Vienna, Austria. During my time working there I deep dived into the hotel tech. world and got hooked. I love working in this field and look forward to new challenges in future...

Why did I start to code?

As I just quit my job to relocate to Kenya I was looking forward learning something new. From a podcast I was listening to (Eastern African Business Podcast) I learned about Moringa School and and Nairobi’s prominent positions as a current/future technology hub on the African continent.

I wanted to know more about the energy that is created by the local technology community and in the same time broaden my own horizon.

I do not know yet if I will ever actually pursue a career as a developer. However, knowing about what drives digitalization and processes behind our data driven future is an essential skill in my opinion and as important as literacy.
